# WhoElse — Content & Orchestration

## 1. Product story (5 bullets)
- You say something once.
- Different AIs hear slightly different words. That's fine.
- What matters is the shared meaning, not the wording.
- WhoElse locks that meaning into one intent.
- Then it dispatches the work — to AI agents, apps, APIs, or people.

## 2. First-screen copy (≤12 words/line)
> **Say it once.**
> We'll figure out what you meant.
>
> *[ mic button ]*
> Speak or type.

Empty-state hint under input:
> Try: "Book me a dentist next week."

## 3. Core flow (4 states)
1. **Listen** — one input (voice or text). No settings, no options visible.
2. **Meaning** — show the single agreed intent as one plain sentence. "You want: ___. Right?"
3. **Dispatch** — pick who does it: AI agent · app · API · human. One tap each.
4. **Result** — show what happened, in one line per handler. Re-say to redo.

## 4. Demo scenarios (exact text to try)
- **Errand:** "Remind me to call mom on Sunday."
- **Research:** "Find three quiet cafes near me with wifi."
- **Delegate:** "Draft a reply telling Sam I'll be 10 minutes late."

Each shows the same meaning captured once, then dispatched to a different handler type.

## 5. Avoid (so it stays a product, not a dashboard)
- No protocol/agent jargon on screen (no "orchestrator", "provider", "schema").
- No config panels before first use.
- No showing raw transcripts side-by-side to prove they differ — just show the agreed meaning.
- No logs, latency, or token counts in the main flow.
- No more than one primary action per screen.
- Copy stays under a breath — if a line needs a comma-heavy clause, cut it.

## 6. Name / tagline
**WhoElse**
> *Say it once. It gets done.*

Alt tagline: *One meaning. Many hands.*
